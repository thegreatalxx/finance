# High-Interest Savings Accounts: Best Rates in Canada

By The Editors

Updated May 15, 2025

---

High-interest savings accounts offer significantly better returns than traditional bank savings accounts, making them essential tools for Canadians looking to maximize their savings. Understanding how these accounts work, where to find the best rates, and how to use them effectively helps you earn more on your money while maintaining access.

High-interest savings accounts differ from regular savings accounts primarily in the interest rates offered. While traditional bank savings accounts might offer 0.1% to 0.5% interest annually, high-interest accounts can offer 2% to 4% or more. This significant difference translates to substantially more earnings on your savings. The difference is particularly impactful on larger balances.

Digital banks most commonly offer high-interest savings accounts. Institutions like Tangerine, Simplii, and others offer rates that significantly exceed traditional bank rates. These accounts operate entirely online, allowing the institutions to offer better rates by avoiding the costs of branch networks. The rates are variable and can change as market conditions change.

Promotional rates are often available for new account holders. These promotional rates might be significantly higher than regular rates, sometimes reaching 4% to 5% for limited periods. These promotions can provide valuable opportunities to earn higher returns. Understanding when promotional rates end helps in planning when to move funds.

Regular rates after promotional periods are still typically higher than traditional bank rates. The post-promotion rates vary by institution and market conditions. Checking the regular rate, not just the promotional rate, ensures you understand what to expect over time. Some institutions maintain competitive rates after promotions, while others drop significantly.

Account accessibility remains a key advantage of high-interest savings accounts. Unlike term deposits or GICs, these accounts allow regular access to your funds. While some transaction limits may apply, the access is generally similar to regular savings accounts. This combination of accessibility and higher interest makes these accounts ideal for emergency funds and short-term goals.

The interest calculation on high-interest savings accounts typically works the same as other savings accounts. Interest accrues daily on your balance and is paid monthly. The annual interest rate divided by 365 gives the daily rate, which is applied to your balance each day. This daily calculation means your interest grows consistently throughout the month.

Tax implications of high-interest savings accounts follow standard rules. Interest earned is taxable income, reported on your annual tax return. This applies whether the interest is paid to you or remains in the account. Tax implications are the same as for other savings accounts. Registered accounts like TFSAs can be used for high-interest savings to shelter interest from tax.

Transaction limits on high-interest savings accounts vary by institution. Some allow unlimited transactions, while others limit monthly transactions to three to six. Understanding these limits prevents surprises and allows appropriate planning. If you anticipate needing frequent access, look for accounts with appropriate transaction allowances.

ATM access for high-interest savings accounts typically requires linked chequing accounts. You cannot access high-interest savings directly at ATMs; you must transfer to a chequing account first. The transfer process is typically instant between accounts at the same institution, allowing quick access to funds. This linking provides the convenience of ATM access while keeping savings in high-interest accounts.

Rate comparison requires ongoing attention as rates change. Checking rates periodically ensures you are earning competitive returns. Rates can increase or decrease based on market conditions and individual institution decisions. Setting up rate alerts or periodically reviewing your options helps maintain competitive earnings.

Account switching can maximize returns when better rates become available. Moving funds from one high-interest account to another allows you to take advantage of competitive rates. The effort to open new accounts and transfer funds can provide meaningful increases in earnings. The benefits are most significant for larger balances.

Direct deposit capabilities allow regular contributions to high-interest savings. Setting up automatic transfers from each paycheque builds savings consistently. This automation ensures regular saving without requiring manual effort. The "pay yourself first" approach works well with automatic contributions.

Combining registered and non-registered accounts provides tax-efficient savings. Using TFSAs for high-interest savings shelters interest from tax, particularly beneficial for those in higher tax brackets. RRSP accounts also provide tax sheltering, though withdrawals are taxed. The specific approach depends on individual circumstances and goals.

Emergency fund suitability makes high-interest savings accounts ideal for this purpose. The combination of competitive interest, accessibility, and stability makes them perfect for emergency savings. Your emergency fund can earn meaningful interest while remaining accessible. This approach is far better than keeping emergency funds in low-interest accounts.

Goals beyond emergency funds can also benefit from high-interest savings. Short-term goals like vacations, home improvements, or major purchases within a few years work well with these accounts. The interest earnings exceed what traditional savings provide while maintaining access. For longer-term goals, investment accounts typically provide better returns.
